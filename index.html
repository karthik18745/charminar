History of the Place

Charminar, an iconic monument located in the heart of Hyderabad, was built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the 5th ruler of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. It was constructed to commemorate the founding of Hyderabad and as a symbol of the eradication of a plague that had devastated the city. The Charminar stands at the intersection of the historical trade routes that connected the Golconda Kingdom to other regions, making it a significant cultural and commercial hub during its time. The structure is renowned for its Indo-Islamic architecture, with each of its four grand arches facing the cardinal directions and topped with four soaring minarets.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Charminar is from November to February. During these months, the weather in Hyderabad is cooler and more pleasant, making it ideal for exploring this historical monument.

Travel Routes

To reach Charminar, you can take the following routes:

  • From Hyderabad Airport: Take a cab or bus towards the old city, approximately 20 km away.
  • From Secunderabad Railway Station: Charminar is around 7 km away; you can take an auto-rickshaw or a bus.
  • From Hyderabad Bus Stand: Various local buses and autos are available to reach Charminar, which is about 5 km from the bus stand.
